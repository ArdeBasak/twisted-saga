Peace Out

In the depths of the night, When the world is still and quiet,
And the stars above twinkle bright, A shiver runs down my spine.

For I know that lurking nearby, In the shadows just out of sight,
Are forces of darkness and fright, Waiting to pounce and strike.

Peace out, they say, with a sneer, As they plot and plan and conspire,
To tear apart what we hold dear, And set our world ablaze with fire.

But we will not be cowed or scared, For we know the power of ferocity,
And we will stand together, prepared, To defend our precious commodity.

With courage in our hearts and minds, And a fierce determination to fight,
We will push back against these dark designs, And keep our world bathed in light.

So let them come, these agents of fear, With their twisted agendas and hate,
For we will face them without a tear, And send them packing, with no debate.

For we are the guardians of peace, And we will not rest until we have won,
Against all those who seek to fleece, The very fabric of what we call home.

Peace out, we say, with a smile, As we celebrate our hard-earned victory,
And we know that, for a little while, Our world will be safe and free.
